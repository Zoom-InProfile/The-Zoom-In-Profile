What's Your Stress Type?

The Zoom-In Profile

by Robin Engelman, PhD

Understand your stress in a way that actually changes how you respond.

We try to manage stress without understanding the pattern driving it. Your stress type is that pattern — a roadmap of how your system responds under pressure. Once you can see it, you can catch it earlier and respond with more clarity.

Stress doesn't just overwhelm you, it changes how you see. Your focus narrows, and you zoom in on what feels wrong.

You assume what you're seeing is accurate, and it shapes how you respond.

Then you try to fix the feeling, while the pattern causing it stays hidden.

This isn't random. It's a pattern. Your pattern. You have countless ways to manage stress. What you've never had is a clear map of where it comes from. That's what this profile gives you.

Understanding the Nine Stress Type Categories

Your results reflect patterns in how you are responding to stress right now. They are not a diagnosis or a complete picture of your mental health, and they do not define you.

It is common for some of these patterns to feel familiar or even uncomfortable to see clearly. That does not mean something is wrong — it means something meaningful is coming into view. If what comes up feels difficult to manage on your own, consider reaching out to a licensed therapist or someone you trust.

This tool is not monitored and responses are not reviewed unless you choose to share them. It does not provide real-time support.

If you are in immediate distress or feel at risk of harming yourself or others, please call 911, go to your nearest emergency room, or contact the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline by dialing 988.
