What's Your Stress Type?

The Zoom-In Profile

by Robin Engelman, PhD

Discover where you are narrowing under pressure, and where you're stronger than you think.

Stress doesn't just make you feel overwhelmed, it changes how you see. Your focus narrows, your thinking shifts, and the tools you know can suddenly feel out of reach.

You start to zoom in on what's wrong.

So you end up trying to fix the feeling, while what's driving it stays out of view.

This isn't random. It follows a pattern.

Most stress tools tell you how much stress you're carrying. This one shows you where it comes from.

In about 10–15 minutes, you'll see:

  • Your stress type and how you narrow under pressure
  • The areas that trigger your stress most quickly
  • Where you're already steady and resilient
  • How your stress type is also a strength — and how to use it to your advantage

Once you understand your stress profile, you can recognize your stress response sooner, slow it down, and respond with clarity in the moments that used to take you out.

Before You Begin

1

Settle

Sit tall, take three slow breaths, letting your exhales run slightly longer than your inhales. This helps your nervous system shift out of immediate stress and into a clearer perspective.

2

Respond

Move through each scenario and choose the response that reflects your most common reaction under stress. Aim for honesty rather than your best-case self. You are capturing how your system actually responds when it narrows.

3

Review

When you finish, you will receive your results. Your scores will highlight where your system tends to narrow under pressure and where it already holds steady.

Current Depletion Level

Right now, how depleted does your nervous system feel overall? Consider sleep, emotional load, mental fatigue, physical strain, and how much you've been carrying.

This helps read your results in context — high depletion can temporarily amplify stress patterns, and your results will reflect that.
